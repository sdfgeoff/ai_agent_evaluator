Post Processing

After successfully printing your part or object on a 3D printer, there are several steps you can take to improve the final product. This process is called post-processing.

  1. Sanding and Filing: Smooth out any rough edges or surfaces with sandpaper, files, or similar tools.

    Note: Be very careful not to remove too much material as this may affect the structural integrity of your print.

  2. Painting or Coating: Apply a paint or coating that complements or enhances the object. This can protect it from damage and make it more visually appealing.

    Note: Choose a suitable finish for the application, considering environmental conditions and use.

  3. Cleaning: Remove any support material left on your print using brushes, solvents, etc.

    Note: Ensure to handle cleaning materials safely as some can be toxic or corrosive.

  4. Polishing: If the object needs a high shine, polish it with suitable polishing compounds.

    Note: High-polish finishes may not be practical for functional items and should be used only where appearance is paramount.
