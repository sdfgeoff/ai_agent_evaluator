Calibration Process

  • Bed Leveling: Use the 'bed leveling' tool in Cura or Pronterface to adjust Z-axis height. Recommended method: 3-point leveling with a sheet of paper between the nozzle and bed.
  • Extruder Calibration: Measure print accuracy by printing calibration cubes. Adjust X/Y travel limits in your printer's firmware if necessary.
  • Temperature Settings: Calibrate hotend temperatures for PLA (200-240°C), ABS (230-250°C), and PETG (235-245°C) based on material specifications.

Post-Filament Change Procedure

  1. Clean Nozzle: Use isopropyl alcohol to remove residual filament from the nozzle. Check for clogs in the extruder gear.
  2. Adjust Retraction: Increase retraction distance (12-20mm) and speed (40-60mm/s) to prevent oozing after filament change.
  3. Test Print: Perform a 3D printed calibration cube to verify print quality. Check for under-extrusion or over-extrusion issues.
  4. Update Firmware: Ensure printer firmware is up-to-date with the latest bug fixes and feature enhancements.
