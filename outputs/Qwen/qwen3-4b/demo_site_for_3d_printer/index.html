Introduction to 3D Printers

3D printers, or additive manufacturing devices, create three-dimensional objects by depositing material layer by layer. Common types include FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling), SLA (Stereolithography), and DMLS (Direct Metal Laser Sintering). This guide covers critical technical aspects of 3D printing operations.

Calibration Process

Proper calibration ensures accurate prints. Key steps include:

  • Bed Leveling: Use a piece of paper or probe to adjust the Z-axis height.
  • Nozzle Offset Calibration: Measure and adjust the nozzle position relative to the bed.
  • Printer Settings: Optimize layer height, infill density, and print speed in your slicer software.

Post-Filament Change Procedures

After changing filament, follow these steps to avoid under-extrusion or clogging:

  1. Heat the extruder to 190°C–230°C (depending on material).
  2. Move the nozzle slowly over the previous filament to clear residue.
  3. Calibrate the printer for the new filament type.
  4. Perform a test print to ensure consistent flow and adhesion.
