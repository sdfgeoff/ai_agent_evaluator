Changing Filament: Best Practices and Process

When it's time to change the filament, following the correct procedures ensures that your printer operates at peak performance and reduces the risk of clogs or print failures. Below you'll find a detailed process:

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Pre-Change Preparation:
    • Cool Down: Allow the extruder and hotend to cool down if you're switching from a high-temperature filament, or heat up the extruder if the filament needs to be removed at printing temperature.
    • Retraction: Retract the filament carefully to prevent any oozing or stringing, minimizing potential jams.
  2. Filament Removal:
    • Once retracted, carefully pull the filament out of the extruder. Some printers provide a dedicated filament removal mode.
  3. Loading the New Filament:
    • Trim the tip of the new filament to create a clean edge for smoother insertion.
    • Insert the new filament slowly into the feeder, ensuring that it slides easily into the hotend assembly.
    • Using controls on your printer, advance the filament until you see a consistent flow of material. This is typically an "extruder priming" step.
  4. Post-Change Calibration:
    • Extrusion Test: Run an extrusion test to verify that the new filament flows correctly.
    • Nozzle Check: Inspect the nozzle for any clogs or remnants from the previous filament.
    • Adjust Settings: Depending on the new filament’s properties (temperature, flow rate, etc.), adjust your printer settings accordingly.
  5. Resuming Print Jobs:
    • After confirming the filament change is successful, resume your print. Monitor the first layers to ensure proper adhesion and flow.

Following this process minimizes downtime and maximizes print quality. With each filament type (PLA, ABS, PETG, etc.) having specific requirements, always refer to the manufacturer’s guidelines in addition to these best practices.
